C++ PROGRAMMING LANGUAGE

WHAT IS C++?

Usually used for object oriented programming, C++ is an improved C language. It dates back to over thirty years to its roots. Although it is far from the oldest programming language, nowadays it is one of the older ones that is in common use, so you might say it gets an A for its ability to respond to changing technical times.

WHAT IS C++ USED FOR?

C++ is used heavily in software engineering for embedded devices. In communications and games, it's also popular. It is used in many other fields, including medicine, banking, and even security. Among the desired abilities, Facebook has posted advertisements that cite C++. For those versed in C++, other recent posts come from Verizon Wireless, WSFS Bank, and Lord & Taylor. One reason why C++ is preferred by programmers is that it integrates well with other languages. Another advantage is that it is high quality.

LEARN C++:

For learning C++, there are other sites you can turn to. Three places have been suggested recently by MakeUseof. LearnCPlusPlus is touted as a guide for a good beginner. An open tutorial with quizzes and practice questions is given by CProgramming. It is written in a welcoming manner and opens with the basics such as a compiler set-up. Also, you'll find basic errors for beginners. You can get a book from the author if you like the theme.

C++ founder Stroustrup emphasizes that even his own website is not a textbook replacement. C++ (depending on who you ask) can be easier to understand than C, but there's a lot to it. The C++ Programming Language is Stroustrup's own book. It's meant for programmers, and might not be suitable for a newbie. You will find additional book reviews on Stroutsup 's blog.
